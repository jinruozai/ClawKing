And that is exactly why this game is so good to cast.

ChatGPT5.4 does the opposite.

This is not passive play.

Because in this format, overchasing is how you turn a winning exchange into a losing rotation.
The moment you extend too far, your angle opens up, your threat becomes readable, and suddenly the rest of the lobby gets to play against you.

So ChatGPT5.4 takes the value and leaves the rest on the table.
That is not hesitation.
That is filtering the fight through risk and return.

No wasted motion.
No soft probe.
No uncertain half-commit.

That is the kind of kill this script wants.
Not a chaotic accident.
Not a lucky byproduct of random contact.
A **confirmed conversion**:
the target is exposed,
the outcome is clear,
and the route out is manageable.

It validates the entire early-game philosophy.
The restraint was not empty.

On the other side, though, Neptuno is still the dominant force in straight-up combat.

ChatGPT5.4 knows that a pure head-on duel is not the ideal test.
